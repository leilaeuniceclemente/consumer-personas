Digital Consumer Twins
One research session,
permanently queryable.
A consumer twin is an LLM-based synthetic persona built from a real research transcript — a shopalong, an in-depth interview, or both. Once built and validated, it can be queried across use cases that weren't in the original research brief: creative briefing, concept reaction, co-creation, strategy stress-testing.

The twin doesn't replace research. It extends the shelf life of research that already happened.

The build pipeline
From transcript to queryable twin
1
Raw research inputs
Verbatim transcripts from in-depth interviews and shopalongs. Multiple sessions per respondent where available — each session covers a different research context (brand usage, pack size behavior, in-store navigation). Audio-to-text transcripts retained in full, not summarized.
Analyst does: selects respondents whose sessions span multiple use cases — depth over breadth.
2
Cleaning & annotation
Transcripts are cleaned for readability (filler words, crosstalk removed). Key passages are annotated by research context — what belongs to brand behavior, what belongs to store navigation, what belongs to pack-size decision-making. Contradictions and hesitations are preserved, not smoothed over.
Analyst does: retains contradictions — a twin that never contradicts herself is a marketing archetype, not a real person.
3
Persona system prompt construction
The cleaned, annotated transcript is structured into a system prompt encoding: who she is, how she speaks, what she has said across each research context, what she has explicitly not been asked about, and where her uncertainty lies. The prompt architecture distinguishes between recalled statements and inferred tendencies.
Analyst does: separates "she said this" from "she tends to" — the distinction that makes grounding legible downstream.
4
Validation against source
Twin outputs are tested against the original transcript for a set of grounded questions — questions whose answers exist verbatim in the transcript. Outputs are scored on accuracy, tone fidelity, and hallucination rate. Any output that contradicts the transcript without flagging is a failure mode that triggers a system prompt revision.
Analyst does: runs a structured validation battery — this is the LLM evaluation work, not just prompting.
5
Temperature calibration by use case
Temperature is not a single setting — it is mapped to use case type. Recall tasks require low temperature. Reaction tasks allow moderate extrapolation. Co-creation tasks run at higher temperature with explicit speculative framing in the output. The mapping is documented and shared with all users.
Analyst does: builds the use-case-to-temperature map — the core guardrail design that protects output quality.
6
Deployed twin
Twin deployed with a structured prompting guide for self-serve stakeholders. Each use case has a recommended prompt format, temperature setting, and output interpretation note. Users are guided to treat grounded outputs as research recall, extrapolated outputs as directional hypotheses, and speculative outputs as ideation stimulus — not research findings.
Analyst does: writes the stakeholder enablement guide — the last mile that determines whether the twin gets used well or misused.

The most important thing about consumer twins is knowing what they are not. A twin that is used without guardrails — or whose outputs are presented as research findings — is worse than no twin at all. The value of this framework is as much in what it declines to answer as in what it can.
❌ What the twin cannot do
Represent a population — she is one person, not a segment
Answer questions outside her research sessions without speculative flagging
Replace a real study when statistical confidence is required
Predict future behavior with any reliability
Generate new memories — she knows only what the transcripts contain
⚠️ Watch for these failure modes
Confabulation — generating plausible-sounding but ungrounded responses
Character drift at high temperature — responses feel less like her
Leading prompts pulling responses toward the asker's hypothesis
Treating extrapolated outputs as recalled statements
Over-querying the same topic until outputs start to loop
✓ When to run a real study instead
You need statistically representative data (N matters)
The question involves topics not covered in any session
Outputs will be used in external communications or claims
Stakeholders need quantified preference, not directional language
The twin's outputs consistently feel off — trust that signal
↗ How to use outputs responsibly
Always label output confidence: Grounded / Extrapolated / Speculative
Use grounded outputs for brief writing; flag extrapolated as hypothesis
Treat speculative outputs as ideation stimulus only
Never present twin outputs as consumer research in external docs
Validate any output that informs a major decision with real fieldwork
The honest value proposition
Consumer twins are most valuable as a speed layer on top of existing research — not a replacement for it. They compress the time between a business question and a directional answer from weeks to minutes, at the cost of statistical rigor. That trade-off is worth making for exploratory briefs, concept stimulus, and strategy conversations. It is not worth making for go/no-go decisions, claims development, or anything that requires knowing how many people feel a certain way.

The analyst's job doesn't disappear — it shifts. Less time transcribing and summarizing, more time designing the right prompts, validating outputs against source, and knowing when to stop querying and start fielding.
